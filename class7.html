1. Mark Zuckerberg

    2. Mark Zuckerberg is the co-founder and CEO of Meta Platforms, the company behind Facebook, Instagram, and WhatsApp. He started Facebook from his college dorm at Harvard when he was just 19 years old. Today, he is leading the development of the metaverse, a digital 3D world where people can interact virtually.

  2. Jeff Bezos

    3. Jeff Bezos is the founder of Amazon, one of the largest online shopping platforms in the world. He started the company from his garage in 1994. Bezos also owns Blue Origin, a space exploration company, and once held the title of the world’s richest person for several years.

  3. Larry Ellison

    4. Larry Ellison is the co-founder of Oracle Corporation, a major software and cloud computing company. Known for his luxurious lifestyle, he owns private islands, yachts, and even a Hawaiian island. Despite being one of the oldest billionaires on the list, he remains actively involved in tech innovation.

  4. Bernard Arnault

    5. Bernard Arnault is the chairman and CEO of LVMH (Moët Hennessy Louis Vuitton), the world’s largest luxury goods company. He controls brands like Louis Vuitton, Dior, Fendi, and more. Arnault is considered the richest person in Europe and a symbol of success in the fashion and luxury world.

  5. Warren Buffett

    6. Warren Buffett is one of the most respected investors in the world and the chairman of Berkshire Hathaway. Known as the “Oracle of Omaha,” he is famous for his simple lifestyle, wisdom in investing, and philanthropy. Buffett has pledged to donate most of his wealth to charitable causes.
